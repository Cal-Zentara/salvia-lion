## The one rule that matters most

**Surprise and curiosity STOP the scroll. Recognition makes them SAVE.**

A cold stranger scrolls past "that's so me" lines. They stop for a jolt: something surprising, a curiosity gap, or a numbered promise. Once they've stopped, recognition ("that's been me for years") is what makes them save it and trust her.

So: open with surprise or curiosity. Deliver recognition inside the post. Never lead with the soft recognition line.

Source: Shortimize Top 10 TikTok Hooks 2024; Social Insider 2026 benchmarks ("validation underperforms curiosity as a scroll-stopper but drives saves").

Bonus: high-arousal emotions (awe, surprise, even a little anger/injustice) get content shared. Calm positivity does not. Source: Jonah Berger virality research, via Upskillist / EMB Global 2025.

## The 6 hook formulas that work (with a Salvia version of each)

### 1. The numbered "signs" list  ← best for us
Why: scannable, predictable value, gets saved most. Stops the scroll AND earns the save.
Source: CoSchedule, HubSpot; numbered format favored by the algorithm.

Salvia versions:
- "3 signs the exhaustion you feel isn't even yours."
- "4 signs you're absorbing other people's energy."
- "3 signs your home is holding energy that isn't yours."

### 2. The counterintuitive claim (surprise)
Why: cognitive friction. The brain has to stop and resolve it.
Source: creator analytics 2025; expectancy-violation psychology.

Salvia versions:
- "You can carry someone else's energy home without realizing it."
- "Your tiredness might not be yours."
- "You don't have to touch someone to absorb their energy."

### 3. "Nobody talks about..." (taboo + curiosity gap)
Why: taboo-breaking + validation. High save rates. ~65-70% watch-through.
Source: Submagic 75 Hooks; Kontentino 2025-2026.

Salvia versions:
- "Nobody tells you that tiredness can belong to someone else."
- "Nobody talks about what you carry home after a hard day."

### 4. The curiosity question
Why: opens a loop the reader has to close. 60-80% hold rate when specific.
Source: Instagram Insights studies via Flick Social, Twicsy.

Salvia versions:
- "Why do you feel drained after certain people, even when nothing happened?"
- "Ever walk into a room and feel something's off before anyone speaks?"

### 5. Insider knowledge
Why: information gap, suggests only the initiated understand.
Source: Dee Higdon Creative 120 Wellness Hooks; Reiki caption libraries.

Salvia versions:
- "Most people don't understand this until they feel it for themselves."
- "If you're feeling stuck, your energy might need clearing, not rest."

### 6. The reframe (subverts expectation)
Why: turns pain into meaning, stops the scroll through contrast.
Source: BuzzVoice, CaptionsAway spiritual libraries.

Salvia versions:
- "That heaviness you can't explain isn't yours to keep."
- "You were never too sensitive. You just feel what others can't."

## What to AVOID on a cover (these don't stop a cold scroll)

- Soft recognition as the opener: "Some of the heaviness you feel was never yours." (Beautiful, but it's a SAVE/retention line, not a scroll-stopper. Use it inside the post, slide 2-3.)
- "A lot of people ask me what Reiki actually is." (An invitation, not a jolt. Fine as slide 2, weak as a cover.)
- Anything calm and pretty with no curiosity gap or surprise.
